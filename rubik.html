Structure of Rubik's Cube
  • On the original classic Rubik's Cube, each of the six faces was covered by nine stickers, each of one of six solid colours: white, red, blue, orange, green, and yellow
  • Some later versions of the cube have been updated to use coloured plastic panels instead, which prevents peeling and fading.
  • Since 1988, the arrangement of colours has been standardised with white opposite yellow, blue opposite green, and orange opposite red, and the red, white, and blue arranged clockwise in that order.
  • On early cubes, the position of the colours varied from cube to cube.
  • For the puzzle to be solved, each face must be returned to have only one colour. Similar puzzles have now been produced with various numbers of sides, dimensions, and stickers, not all of them by Rubik.
